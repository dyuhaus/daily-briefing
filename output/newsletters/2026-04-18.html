Market News

The rally that crossed 7,000 on April 15 has continued without pause. The S&P 500 closed today at 7,125 — up another 1.2% — with the Dow adding 1.79% to 49,447 and the Nasdaq gaining 1.52%. The catalyst hardening today is Iran's formal declaration that the Strait of Hormuz is 'completely open' for commercial traffic, moving the story from peace-deal speculation to a concrete operational fact. WTI crude pulled back further on the news, and Treasuries rallied as oil-driven inflation risk recedes. The Hormuz declaration matters beyond the headline: roughly 20% of global seaborne oil transits that chokepoint, and its reopening directly reduces the supply-shock premium that had been baked into energy and shipping names since the conflict escalated.

Two countercurrents worth watching. First, the IMF's April 2026 World Economic Outlook cut global growth to 3.1% for 2026 — a meaningful downgrade that reflects the cumulative drag from the trade war earlier in the year and conflict disruptions, even as equity markets price in a soft landing. The gap between IMF macro pessimism and equity market euphoria is historically a late-cycle signal. Second, reporting flagged that the Fed is internally debating an April rate hike — described as 'unthinkable' months ago — because tariff-driven goods inflation has not fully unwound despite the U.S.-China truce. If the FOMC actually moves in April, it would be the first hike into a geopolitical shock since 1994 and would reprice the entire risk rally instantly. A third dynamic: retail traders have surged back into equities, Bloomberg reporting they are driving outsized intraday moves in individual names. High retail participation at record index levels is a known liquidity amplifier on both the upside and any sudden reversal.

Key Developments
The Strait of Hormuz 'completely open' declaration is a concrete bullish catalyst, not just sentiment — watch tanker ETFs and oil volatility (OVX) for confirmation that the risk premium is fully unwinding. A sustained WTI move below $90 would suggest the market believes it.
Fed April hike risk is not priced. If this materializes, bonds and rate-sensitive equities reprice hard and fast. On Kalshi, any 'Fed hikes in April' contract is likely mispriced given how little attention this scenario is getting in equity land.
The IMF's 3.1% global growth forecast versus S&P 7,125 creates a widening credibility gap. The IMF has historically lagged turning points, but when the gap is this large it tends to resolve via market correction rather than IMF upgrade — size positions accordingly.
Retail trader re-entry at all-time highs historically compresses the remaining upside window before a consolidation. Momentum strategies that have worked for 12 consecutive Nasdaq sessions may be entering diminishing-returns territory; consider tighter trailing stops on trend-following positions.

OpenAI News

The two genuinely new threads today are on the API and security sides. Microsoft made a significant structural move by deprecating both AutoGen and Semantic Kernel as active development projects, shifting them to maintenance-only mode and replacing them with a unified Microsoft Agent Framework that bundles governance tooling for autonomous agents. This is a notable signal — two of the most widely adopted agent scaffolding libraries in enterprise settings are effectively being retired in favor of a single, governance-first successor. For teams building production agent systems, this is a forced migration decision to plan around.

The repository represents one of the most active community-driven agent memory and tool-use frameworks outside of the major labs. Sourcegraph's Cody also restructured its Free, Pro, and Enterprise Starter tiers this week, suggesting the coding assistant market is entering a pricing and packaging maturation phase. A quieter but technically interesting release is Open-dLLM, a diffusion-based language model architecture specifically targeting code generation — an experimental departure from the autoregressive norm that is worth watching if it gains traction.
