Market News

Wednesday's session was a tale of two markets. Early selling — S&P 500 down 0.37% to 6,556, Nasdaq off 0.84%, Dow shedding roughly 84 points — gave way to a late recovery push with the index clawing back to roughly flat-to-up 0.75% by end of day, extending what has been a choppy weekly rebound. The primary catalyst for the reversal was geopolitical: President Trump reportedly transmitted a 15-point peace plan to Tehran, sparking de-escalation optimism even as Iran formally rejected an initial ceasefire proposal and continued military operations. Oil pulled back roughly four dollars to $88.48 on the news — a meaningful release valve given how much the energy spike has been complicating the Fed's inflation calculus. The Federal Reserve held benchmark rates steady at its March meeting, as expected, with Powell characterizing the oil shock's economic effects as likely temporary. That framing is doing a lot of work right now — markets are pricing it as dovish cover, but the dual shock to growth and prices documented in this week's data makes that confidence fragile.

The broader macro backdrop remains uncomfortable. Tariffs from 2025 are showing measurable short-run drag per Yale Budget Lab data, commodity prices are on a World Bank-projected path to six-year lows outside of energy, and trade policy uncertainty is suppressing corporate planning horizons. The New York Fed's DSGE model update for March adds to a picture of slowing but not collapsing growth. For prediction market and algo trading purposes, the Iran situation is the dominant binary: a credible ceasefire agreement would likely send oil down another five-plus dollars fast, bid risk assets meaningfully, and shift the Fed's posture — whereas a breakdown in talks or escalation would push energy back toward $95 and re-price rate cut timelines. The market is currently oscillating on each diplomatic headline, which creates clean mean-reversion setups around geopolitical news flow.

Key Developments
The US-Iran ceasefire negotiation is the highest-leverage macro binary right now — oil at $88.48 is priced for cautious optimism, and a confirmed deal versus a breakdown represents a roughly $5-10 swing in crude with downstream equity and rate effects. Prediction markets on ceasefire resolution should be actively monitored for edge.
Powell's 'temporary effects' framing on the oil shock is the market's permission slip to stay risk-on despite sticky inflation — watch for any Fed speakers this week who push back on that framing, as a dissent would be a significant re-pricing catalyst for rate cut timeline markets.

OpenAI News

The Sora Team announced the discontinuation via X, citing three compounding failures: low user adoption, unresolved intellectual property disputes, and deepfake-related ethical concerns that proved too difficult to contain at scale. For a product OpenAI once positioned as a flagship creative offering, this is a notable retreat. Separately, OpenAI is in active model consolidation mode — GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and o4-mini are being retired from ChatGPT as the GPT-5 family takes over. On the research side, OpenAI introduced Prism, a LaTeX-native scientific writing workspace powered by GPT-5.2, alongside a published study on using GPT-5 to accelerate scientific discovery — signaling a deliberate push into professional and academic research workflows.

General AI Industry Updates
Heavy day

Meta's Llama 4 family is the week's biggest open-source story. The two flagship variants — Maverick (17B active parameters out of roughly 400B total, using 128 experts) and Scout (notable for an industry-leading 10-million token context window) — are the company's first open-weight models built on Mixture-of-Experts architecture and designed as natively multimodal from the ground up. Both are live on Hugging Face. This is a meaningful architectural break from Llama 3 and positions Meta's open stack closer to what closed frontier models offer in terms of context depth. Separately, Alibaba's QwenLM has Qwen3.5 appearing on GitHub, continuing the steady cadence of competitive open-source releases from Chinese labs.

GitHub Copilot pushed a concentrated wave of agentic expansions: a Jira integration enabling autonomous PR generation from ticket assignments, an agentic code review architecture, ACP support in the Copilot CLI, and expanded JetBrains agentic capabilities — several of these hitting public preview or GA simultaneously.
